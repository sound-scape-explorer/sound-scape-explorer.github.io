## Preparing for exploration

- Start the <SseIcon /> standalone app
- In the `Audio` section, either
  - Fill the audio path by inputting one of your files
    (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>1</span>)
  - Or enter the path manually
    (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>2</span>)
- Click on the start button
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>3</span>)
- The audio engine will start
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>4</span>)
- Allowing you to click
  on the `Visualisation > Explore` button
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>5</span>)

## Loading your dataset

A new visualisation window opens after clicking on the `Explore` button.

<figure>
  ![](/sse-14/visualisation/003.png)
  <figcaption>
    <center>
      <i>Visualisation app default Open screen</i>
    </center>
  </figcaption>
</figure>

One more final step...

Choose your `.h5` and load it.

The Open panel will close and the View one will open.

<figure>
  ![](/sse-14/visualisation/004.png)
  <figcaption>
    <center>
      <i>Visualisation app View panel</i>
    </center>
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This is where you select your:

- Extraction
- Band
- Integration
- Reducer

Once your selection done, the View panel automatically closes and
data starts loading.

:::info
Depending on your dataset size, data can take minutes to load entirely.
:::

## Exploring

Data loading is complete!

You can now see the scatter plot.

<figure>
  ![](/sse-14/visualisation/005.png)
  <figcaption>
    <center>
      <i>Visualisation app with scatter plot loaded</i>
    </center>
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This screen has three main parts:

- The scatter
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>1</span>)
- The camera controls and quick actions
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>2</span>)
- The panel buttons
  (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>3</span>) ([reference](../reference/visualisation-panels/))

:::info
In the center, the scatter (<span style={{color: 'red'}}>1</span>) plots
points, either in 2D or 3D depending on your reducer setting.

Each of these points represents an **audio interval**, with the length
of your `Integration` value.

The underlying represented data for each interval
depends on your actual `Extractor` setting.

This can be either neural network embeddings that have been reduced,
or a combination of them, or even with raw or acoustic indices mixed in.
:::
